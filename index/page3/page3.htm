When biking from the Killarney end, at the end of your journey lies the pristine Wekiwa Springs National Park, where you can experience swimming, kayaking, and fishing in the waters of the Wekiwa River, hiking along the trails that lead through woods and sandhill uplands, and even camping overnight. The springs pump out an incredible 42 million gallons daily, and the water is always at 72 degrees F, perfect for cooling down on a hot day. The area was inhabited by Seminole natives, with "Wekiwa" translating to "spring of water" in the Creek language. The area became an attraction in the 1860s, when it was called Clay Springs after the property owner. The property was bought by the Apopka Sportsman Club in 1941 for logging, hunting, and fishing, and was again sold to the state of Florida in 1969, when it was made a state park. Wekiwa Springs is also home to the Florida Park Service Ranger Academy, moved there from O'Leno State Park in 1977.

Pricing

Entry and Rental Pricing

Entry into the park is $4.00 for a vehicle with one person, or $6.00 for a vehicle with multiple. Children under six are admitted for free. The park is open from 8 AM until sundown, and is open 365 days a year. Bicycles are avaliable for rental at $10.00 an hour or $60.00 per day. Canoes and single kayaks can be rented for $35.00 for two hours, while double and triple kayaks, as well as paddleboards, are $40.00 for two hours. Any watercraft can be rented overnight for $90.00 first night, and $45.00 each additional night. All watercraft excluding overnight rentals must be returned before closing; an additional charge of $1.00 per minute will be applied to those returning after closing.

Camping Pricing

Campers can either stay with a standard experience for $24.00 per night, plus a $6.70 reservation fee and $7.00 nightly utility fee, or the "primitive" experience, which is secluded from the rest of the park (site pictured below) and offers few amenities for an authentic camping experience, for just $5.00 per person per night. If camping with a youth group, pricing is $1.00 per youth per night, and $5.00 per adult chaperone per night. Campers should note that the Florida Black Bear can be found at the park, and should review the Bear Awareness form to stay safe.
